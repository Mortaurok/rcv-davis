Ranked-Choice Voting: A Better Way to Vote

What is Ranked-Choice Voting?

Ranked-choice voting lets you rank candidates in order of preference — 1st, 2nd, 3rd, etc. If your top choice can’t win, your vote counts for your next choice. It’s just a smarter, more fair way to vote.

Why We Need It

  • Tired of voting for the “lesser evil”?
  • Sick of winners getting only 30% of the vote?
  • Want to vote your conscience without wasting your vote?
Ranked-choice voting makes sure every vote counts—and every winner has real majority support.

Common Questions

Question Answer
Isn’t RCV too confusing? If you can rank pizza toppings, you can rank candidates. It’s intuitive.
Does it help one party? No. RCV is nonpartisan. Republicans, Democrats, and independents win fairly.
Isn’t this too radical? It’s used in Maine, Alaska, NYC, San Francisco—and even Utah cities. It works.

Fair Elections Are American

This isn’t about left or right. It’s about a voting system that reflects what the people really want. RCV gives you more voice, more choice, and real representation.
